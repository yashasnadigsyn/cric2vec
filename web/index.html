Cric2Vec: Cricket in High-Dimensional Space

Learning dense vector representations of cricketers to decode playing style, role, and similarity.

In the world of sports analytics, we are often drowning in aggregates: averages, strike rates, and economy rates. While useful, these metrics flatten the complexity of the game. They tell us what happened, but rarely how or why.

Cric2Vec is an attempt to move beyond aggregates. By treating a cricket match not as a spreadsheet of numbers but as a semantic sequence of events—much like language—we can use techniques from Natural Language Processing to learn rich, multi-dimensional representations of players.

The Core Concept: Word2Vec for Cricket

What is an Embedding?

Computers cannot understand raw text (like "King") or entities (like "Virat Kohli"). Traditional methods map these to simple ID numbers, but IDs have no meaning—ID #45 isn't "closer" to ID #46 than ID #100.

Embeddings solve this by mapping every entity to a vector of numbers (coordinates) in a high-dimensional space. In this space, mathematical distance equals semantic similarity.

# "Virat Kohli" as a dense vector used by neural nets [ 0.82, -0.45, 0.12, 0.99, ... ]

The Language Analogy

Word2Vec revolutionised NLP with a simple insight: "You shall know a word by the company it keeps." (The Distributional Hypothesis). The word "Banking" appears in similar contexts to "Finance", so their vectors move closer together during training.

Cric2Vec applies this exact logic to Cricket:

Natural Language (NLP) ➜ Cricket (Cric2Vec)
Sentence ➜ Over / Match Sequence
Word ➜ Event (Batter + Bowler + Outcome)
Grammar ➜ Rules of Cricket
Semantic Context ➜ Game State (Powerplay, Death Overs)

Just as King - Man + Woman = Queen works in word vectors, we find that Kohli - Anchor + Finisher ≈ Dhoni holds true in our cricket vectors.

The Inspiration

This project builds directly upon the foundational ideas introduced in two key research papers: Batter|Pitcher2Vec and NBA2Vec. These works demonstrated that the "distributional hypothesis" in linguistics—that a word is characterized by the company it keeps—applies equally well to athletes.

Batter|Pitcher2Vec

This research brought the Word2Vec architecture to Major League Baseball. By treating the batter-pitcher duel as a predictive task, the model learned representations that captured subtle nuances of gameplay. For instance, without being explicitly told about pitch types, the model grouped pitchers who relied on heavy movement together. It proved that a neural network could verify baseball intuition (e.g., specific lefty-righty matchups) purely from the raw sequence of at-bats.

NBA2Vec

Expanding on this significantly, NBA2Vec modeled the more chaotic, 10-player environment of basketball. It predicted possession outcomes based on the entire lineup on the court. The critical insight here was that "positions" (Point Guard, Center) are often fluid. The embeddings revealed that players cluster by role and playing style rather than their nominal position on the scorecard.

From Court to Pitch

Cric2Vec takes these concepts to the cricket pitch. Cricket is uniquely suited for this: it combines the dyadic nature of the batter-bowler contest (like Baseball) with the broader team context and conditions (like Basketball).

A Note on Intent: It is crucial to clarify what this project is not. We are not building a betting engine. We are not trying to predict the winner of the next IPL or World Cup match. If you want to know who will win, look at the odds. If you want to know who a player is—mathematically, stylistically, and fundamentally—that is what Cric2Vec answers. We are decoding the DNA of playing style.

Methodology & Architecture

Our core model, Cricket2VecV2, learns by attempting to predict the outcome of every ball (dot, single, boundary, wicket) given the context. We move beyond simple batter-bowler interactions by incorporating Team Embeddings (capturing tactical styles like CSK's spin choke) and Venue Embeddings (distinguishing between the small boundaries of Chinnaswamy and the spin-friendly Chepauk).

Beyond The Embeddings

The true power of Cric2Vec lies in its applications. By performing algebraic operations in this high-dimensional space, we unlock capabilities that traditional stats cannot touch.

Dream XI Generator

Using Farthest Point Sampling on the embedding manifold, we can mathematically construct the most "diverse" team possible. Given a captain (e.g., Rohit Sharma), the model hunts for players who occupy orthogonal regions of the vector space, ensuring that every tactical base—from anchor to pinch-hitter—is covered.

Matchup Heatmaps

Historical data is sparse. Kohli may have only faced Archer for 10 balls. Cric2Vec fills in the gaps. By simulating thousands of deliveries between any batter and bowler using their learned vectors, we generate dense probability heatmaps for every possible matchup, revealing hidden vulnerabilities.
